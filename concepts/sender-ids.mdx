title: "Sender IDs"
description: "How sender IDs and shortcodes work, and how to register your brand."
---

The `shortcode` field on every send request controls **what the
recipient sees as the sender** of your SMS. It can be one of:

| Type            | Example  | Notes                                             |
| --------------- | -------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Alphanumeric    | `XYRA`   | 1–11 characters, letters and digits, no spaces.   |
| Numeric shortcode | `22000` | 5-digit Kenyan shortcodes, allocated per service. |

## Default sender

If you don't have a registered sender ID yet, you can send test traffic
using `XYRA`. This is fine for development, but **production messages
should always use your own registered sender** so recipients recognise
the brand.

## Registering a branded sender ID

To register a sender ID for your business (e.g. `ACME`, `KARIBU`):

1. Choose your sender ID. **Must be 1–11 characters**, letters and
   digits only, no spaces, no punctuation.
2. Email [hello@xyratechnology.co.ke](mailto:hello@xyratechnology.co.ke) with:
   - Your business name and registration number.
   - Sample message content (one or two examples of what you'll send).
   - The sender ID you'd like.
3. Approval normally takes 2–5 business days. Carrier registration is
   handled on your behalf.

<Note>
  Sender IDs are case-insensitive — `XYRA`, `Xyra`, and `xyra` are
  treated as the same registered sender.
</Note>

## Restrictions

- Generic words (`SMS`, `INFO`, `BANK`, `MPESA`) are blocked by carriers.
- Sender IDs that impersonate another brand will be rejected.
- Promotional sender IDs may need additional documentation depending on
  the industry (finance, gaming, health).

## What happens if my sender ID isn't registered?

If you send a message with a `shortcode` that hasn't been registered
with carriers, the carrier will reject the message at the network edge.
You'll see a delivery status of `UNDELIV` or `REJECTD` when you query
the DLR. See [Delivery Receipts](/concepts/delivery-receipts) for what
each status means.
